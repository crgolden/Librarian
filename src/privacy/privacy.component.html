Privacy Policy

Effective 2026-07-17. See the FAQ for a plainer walkthrough of linking your account.

What we collect

When you link your PlayStation account, Curator stores:

  • Your PlayStation account ID
  • An encrypted access token and refresh token, and when they expire
  • Your PlayStation library entitlements, and the derived, curated library built from them
  • Your consoles, install sizes, and any collections you create
  • If you choose to provide one, an encrypted RAWG and/or OpenCritic API key — entirely optional, used only to enrich your own library with genre/score metadata
  • A log of the high-level actions taken on your behalf (see "Your action history" below)

What we never collect

  • Your NPSSO token — used once to obtain your access/refresh tokens, then discarded. It is never written to a database or a log line.
  • Your email address — compared in memory against PlayStation's verified email at link time, then forgotten. There is no email column anywhere in Curator's database.
  • Trophies — read live from PlayStation and cached for 15 minutes, never stored permanently
  • Your presence, friends list, devices, or chat — read live if at all, never stored
  • Your real name, address, phone number, or any payment information

How it's protected

Access and refresh tokens are encrypted before they're stored; only Curator holds the key. If you provide a RAWG or OpenCritic key, it's encrypted with that same key. Every connection to Curator uses TLS. Your NPSSO is used once, in memory, and never persisted.

About RAWG/OpenCritic keys

Curator doesn't provision a shared RAWG or OpenCritic key for every user — it doesn't scale. If you choose to provide your own, it's stored encrypted and used only to enrich your own library; it's never shown back to you or anyone else after you save it, and you can remove it at any time from your PSN settings page.

If your key retrieves data Curator doesn't already have for a game (its genre, its critic score), that game metadata — never your key — is added to Curator's shared game catalog, so future lookups for that same game, yours or another user's, don't need to call RAWG/OpenCritic again. This is why some games in your library may already show enrichment data even if you've never provided a key yourself — someone else's key (or Curator's own periodic catalog refresh) already resolved it.

How long we keep it

Your library, tokens, and account data are kept until you unlink or delete your account. Trophy reads are cached for 15 minutes and expire on their own. Your action history (below) is kept for one year, including for a period after you delete your account — see "Your action history."

Your controls

From your PSN settings page you can, at any time:

  • Unlink — immediately deletes your stored PlayStation tokens
  • Delete my data — immediately deletes your account and everything associated with it: your PSN link, library, consoles, collections, any saved preferences, and any RAWG/OpenCritic keys. This is instant and self-service; you don't need to email anyone to make it happen.
  • Remove a RAWG/OpenCritic key — independent of the above; removes just that key, immediately, without touching anything else in your account.

Your action history

If your PlayStation account were ever compromised by something unrelated to Curator and you needed to know exactly what Curator itself did with your account, this is how we — and you — can check. Curator keeps a log of the high-level actions it takes on your behalf: linking attempts (and why one failed, if it did), unlinking, library refresh requests, trophy fetches, and account deletion. It never contains your NPSSO, your tokens, or raw data pulled from PlayStation — only which action happened and when.

You can view and download this history yourself at any time from your PSN settings page. Because its purpose is to answer disputes about what happened, this log is kept for one year and is not deleted immediately when you delete your account — it outlives the "Delete my data" action for that one-year window, then is purged automatically.

Open source

Every claim on this page is verifiable in public code — we'd rather you check than take our word for it. Librarian (this site and its BFF) is at github.com/crgolden/Librarian. Curator (the API that handles your NPSSO, verifies your email, and stores your data) is at github.com/crgolden/Curator. A few specific places worth pointing to directly: token encryption lives in persistence/crypto.py, linking (including the never-persisted-NPSSO / in-memory email match) is in link_service.py, the no-email-column schema tenet is documented in db/migrations/0001_initial.sql, the action-history log described above is db/migrations/0003_account_action_log.sql, and the RAWG/OpenCritic key storage/routes described above are db/migrations/0004_user_enrichment_keys.sql and enrichment_keys_routes.py.
